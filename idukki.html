IDUKKI

Idukkiis beautiful High range district of Kerala is geographically known for its Mountainous Hills and Dense Forests. Also known as the “land of dams, lakes & rivers”, the district of Idukki comprises of a number of dams and reservoirs namely, the famous Idukki dam (the highest arch dam in Asia) and the adjoining Cheruthoni & Kulamavu dams, the Mullaperiyar dam, Malankara dam, Mattupetty dam, Kundala dam, Anayirangal dam, Kallarkutty dam and the Boothathankettu dam, to name the most important dams. Idukki District is also rightly called the “land of spices” with a large area under the cultivation of Cardamom, Pepper, Cloves, Ginger, Cinnamon and Nutmeg. For the people of Kerala, Idukki is always associated with Power Generation. About 66% of the State’s Power needs come from the Hydroelectric Power Projects in Idukki. Idukki accounts for 12.9 percent of the area of Kerala and only 3.7 percent of the population of Kerala. The district has been abundantly blessed by Mother Nature, dotted with a number of touristic attractions. The natural beauty of the district makes aptly makes it the “Paradise” in “Gods own Country” – Kerala or rather “God’s Own District”. Idukki district was formed on 26 January 1972.The district consists of Devikulam, Udumbanchola and Peermedu taluks of the erstwhile Kottayam district and Thodupuzha taluk (excluding two villages Manjallore and Kalloorkadu) of the erstwhile Ernakulam district. At the time of formation the district headquarters started functioning at Kottayam and from there it was shifted to Painavu in Thodupuzha taluk in June 1976.
